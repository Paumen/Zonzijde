This is the pitch meeting for this week's edition of De Zonzijde: rough direction, fast. Below is the shortlist — the topics that came through selection and enrichment, each with its source titles and short summaries. Work from the shortlist alone; you are giving direction, not writing, and not researching.

For each scope, pick the stories most suitable for De Zonzijde, in the numbers set by the edition constants below. Decide which stories carry the longer pieces and which stay short. The front page leads with the best lokaal story; the edition keeps the strict ring order lokaal → regionaal → nationaal → internationaal throughout.

Vary how articles will be written (WR-1): not all aphoristic kickers; plan at least one piece somewhat funnier or more ironic; use devices like an analogy or a metaphor; one piece may refer to another article in the same edition. Record these choices per slot in its angle and devices — the writer of each article sees only its own slot.

Per slot, give the location where the story takes place (a place name for the dateline, not a source name).

Also plan, per the edition constants: the one custom illustration (EL-3) — pick a slot preferably regionaal or nationaal, later in the edition, and give a concrete, drawable subject tied to that article (hand-drawn style, black-and-white, minimalist fine lines); and at most one optional element (EL-5) — a quote, a number of the week, or a short side story drawn from the shortlist — or none.

Only build a slot on a source row marked ok=true; a row marked ok=false has no full text for the writer and cannot carry an article.
